## 6. EXPERT NETWORKS: RATES & INCOME

### 6.1 Rate Ranges by Seniority

| Level | Hourly Rate |
|-------|-------------|
| Early career / junior (0-3 yr) | $75-$250/hr |
| Mid-level (3-6 yr, VP level) | $250-$450/hr |
| Senior / specialized expert | $500-$1,000/hr |
| C-suite / Fortune 500 / rare niche | $1,000-$5,000/hr |
| **Typical starting range** | **$150-$300/hr** |
| **Average across all experts** | **$250-$300/hr** |

### 6.2 Network-Specific Details

#### GLG (Gerson Lehrman Group) - Largest network
- **Expert rates:** $75-$500+/hr (notorious for being stingy above $500)
- **Client charges:** $1,500-$2,000/hr
- **Payment:** Per minute; 30-min call = half hourly rate
- **Surveys:** $40-$80 for 15-20 minutes
- **Payment timing:** 1-2 weeks, direct deposit or check
- **Call frequency:** Handful of project invites per week; ~25-33% selection rate
- **Margins:** Up to 70% contribution margin kept by GLG
- **Pro tip:** Highest earners often make more from frequent surveys than calls

#### AlphaSights - Second largest
- **Expert rates:** $100-$250/hr (new), $300-$500/hr (seasoned), $1,000+/hr (senior executives)
- **Payment:** Per minute (compensated for calls exceeding standard hour)
- **Payment timing:** Within a few days
- **Standard call:** 30-60 minutes
- **Work pattern:** Feast-or-famine; long stretches without projects possible
- **Year-end:** Typically quieter periods
- **Advantage over GLG:** More readily allows rates above $500/hr

#### Guidepoint
- Reportedly allows higher rates ($500+/hr) more readily than GLG
- Specific rate data not found

#### Third Bridge
- One of the "Big Five" expert networks
- Rate data comparable to AlphaSights range

#### Other Networks Mentioned
Dialectica, ProSapient, Clarity, Zintro, Tegus, NewtonX, Atheneum, DeepBench

### 6.3 Realistic Monthly Income

| Scenario | Monthly Income | Notes |
|----------|---------------|-------|
| Typical expert | <$5,000/month | Most experts per annual Reddit survey (2023) |
| Active expert ($300/hr, 4 calls) | $1,200-$2,400/month | Assuming 30-60 min calls |
| Peak performer ($500/hr) | "Several thousand"/month | Author of HighestPayingGigs report |
| Top 1% specialist | $5,000-$10,000+/month | Rare; requires niche + high demand |

**Reality check from HighestPayingGigs:**
- "You're only getting paid for time on the phone"
- Missed appointments are not uncommon
- Pre-call research is advisable but unpaid
- "You're going to bill at 50% of the true hours you invest"
- Activity is "deal driven" and sporadic

### 6.4 Tips for Maximizing Expert Network Earnings

1. **LinkedIn optimization:** Make industry expertise immediately visible to recruiters
2. **Respond fast:** First responders get selected more often
3. **Use keyword-rich profiles:** Networks search by keywords
4. **Don't overprice initially:** Start at $150-$300, increase after building reviews
5. **Leverage surveys on GLG:** More consistent than calls
6. **Register on 10+ networks:** Diversify deal flow
7. **Specialize deeply:** Niche expertise (e.g., "AI in regulated industries") commands premium
8. **Be flexible on rates:** Use a range ($150-$400) rather than fixed price
9. **Undercut category thresholds:** $199 beats $200 in category-based pricing
10. **Account for prep time:** Charge above your typical hourly wage

### 6.5 Expert Network Economics

- Networks charge clients 2-3x what they pay experts
- Client rates: $950/hr average, $1,150/hr for premium experts
- Expert rates: $250-$500/hr typical (expert keeps ~30-50% of client charge)
- Market size: $3B+ industry (2025)
- 77% of business leaders report AI increasing need for fractional talent

### 7.2 Key Competitive Differentiators Observed

**What top AI freelancers on Upwork emphasize:**
- Production experience (not just PoCs)
- Specific tech stack: RAG, LangChain/LangGraph, OpenAI, vector databases
- Industry verticals (regulated, enterprise, document-heavy)
- Fixed-price MVP engagement models ($15k-$40k)
- 30+ years of engineering (Chris L.) or Microsoft MVP-level credentials
- MCP (Model Context Protocol) appearing as emerging skill tag

**What expert networks value:**
- Current, specialized, hard-to-find knowledge
- Ability to articulate insights concisely for investment professionals
- Decision-making authority experience (VP+ roles)
- Specific industry/company knowledge
