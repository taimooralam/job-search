# Iteration 4.3 Manual Master-CV Review Methodology

## 1. Purpose

This document turns the eval corpus, the 4.2 presentation-contract
implementation, the current CV guide, and current market/ATS guidance into a
manual working method for 4.3.1.

Its job is to answer five practical questions:

1. What truthful CV structure does each target persona family want?
2. What should the header look like in ATS-safe, recruiter-rich, and
   region-specific variants?
3. Where do tagline, identity statement, bullet points, and competencies
   belong?
4. Which personal metadata should be stored, and which should be displayed,
   by market?
5. How should the master CV be manually reviewed so downstream stages can
   tailor aggressively without drifting from truth?

This document is normative for manual 4.3.1 authoring and advisory for
4.3.2 through 4.3.6 implementation.

## 3. What The Eval Corpus Actually Establishes

### 3.1 The 12 target categories collapse into 6 practical persona lanes

| Lane | Categories | Truthful persona | Unsafe drift |
|---|---|---|---|
| Architect, EEA | `ai_architect_eea` | Senior architect or player-coach translating enterprise AI requirements into governed delivery | Executive AI leader, heavy people manager, research specialist |
| Architect, global/GCC | `ai_architect_global`, `ai_architect_ksa_uae` | Solutions/AI architect proving platform design, production delivery, governance, stakeholder translation | Multi-cloud authority without proof, customer-facing authority without proof, Head/Director/VP framing |
| Architect-first AI leadership adjacency | `head_of_ai_eea`, `head_of_ai_global`, `head_of_ai_ksa`, `head_of_ai_uae` | Architect-first player-coach or technical AI platform lead | Literal executive Head of AI, budget/P&L, org-building-heavy identity |
| Staff/Principal AI IC | `staff_ai_engineer_eea`, `staff_ai_engineer_global` | Senior IC who architects and productionizes AI platforms and backend systems | Director/VP, formal management, unsupported framework authority |
| Senior/Tech lead AI IC | `senior_ai_engineer_eea`, `tech_lead_ai_eea` | Hands-on senior builder or technical lead for production AI systems | Executive identity, pure architect/no-coding framing, management inflation |
| Player-coach AI delivery manager | `ai_eng_manager_eea` | Hands-on AI engineering manager with architecture, mentorship, and delivery proof | Product manager, Head of AI, formal org owner without evidence |

### 3.2 The real common denominator is narrower than the job titles

Across the corpus, the safest repeated positioning is:

- architect-first or senior-IC-first;
- production AI platform delivery;
- retrieval, evaluation, guardrails, structured outputs, integration depth;
- reliability, modernization, and operational discipline;
- stakeholder translation and player-coach influence;
- no executive inflation.

This means the master CV should not be authored as 12 separate identities.
It should be authored as one evidence base that can truthfully project into
6 lanes.

### 3.3 Persona-family structure expectations

| Lane | Title family | Summary angle | Key-achievement bias | Competency bias | Experience bias |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Architect | `AI Architect`, `AI Solution Architect`, `Solutions Architect`, `Technical Architect` if AI scope is proven | Enterprise architecture plus production AI delivery plus governance | Platform design, delivery, integration, evaluation, guardrails | Architecture, RAG, agents/orchestration if true, governance, reliability | Current role heavy, greenfield + modernization range visible |
| Head-of-AI adjacency | Conservative lead/architect/principal family, not literal executive title unless directly true | Production AI architecture plus delivery/reliability plus stakeholder influence | System ownership, delivery, reliability, evaluation, mentoring | Architecture, reliability, governance, translation | Player-coach signals visible; no executive-only bullets |
| Staff/Principal IC | `Staff AI Engineer`, `Principal AI Engineer`, `Staff Engineer`, `Principal Engineer` | Architecture-first senior IC with production quality and system influence | Reliability, scale, evaluation, platform decisions, integration depth | Platform engineering, distributed systems, RAG/agents only if true | Recent technical proof prioritized over leadership anecdotes |
| Senior AI engineer | `Senior AI Engineer`, `Senior Applied AI Engineer`, adjacent software title with AI qualifier | Hands-on system builder with shipped AI features | Product integration, RAG, evaluation, delivery speed | Applied AI, backend, product integration | Technical execution bias, lower management signal |
| Tech lead AI | `Tech Lead`, `Technical Lead`, `AI Tech Lead` | Hands-on technical leadership via code, mentoring, delivery discipline | Architecture decisions, production readiness, mentoring | Python/backend/AI systems, quality, mentoring | Current role plus mentoring/design-review proof |
| AI engineering manager | Conservative manager family if evidence exists; otherwise lead/architect | Player-coach delivery manager with platform design and stakeholder alignment | Delivery outcomes, adoption, roadmap translation, mentoring | Architecture, evaluation, governance, delivery | Team enablement visible but bounded |

## 4. Canonical CV Structure

### 4.1 Default section order

This is the default order for all ATS-safe variants unless 4.2.2 explicitly
changes it:

1. Header
2. Summary
3. Key achievements
4. Core competencies
5. Experience
6. Selected projects
7. Education
8. Certifications
9. Languages

Optional:

- `ai_highlights` only when 4.2.2 `ai_section_policy` is `required` or
  strongly `optional` and the evidence pool is rich enough that a separate AI
  block clarifies rather than duplicates.
- `awards` and `publications` only when true and relevant.

### 4.2 Section purpose

| Section | Purpose | What belongs here | What does not belong here |
|---|---|---|---|
| Header | Identity and reachability | name, chosen title, contact links, location, selected policy-approved metadata, proof line, differentiator line | long prose, unsupported skills, sensitive metadata by default |
| Summary | 2 to 4 short sentences that state the hiring thesis | identity statement, scope, strongest proof categories, category-fit framing | tool dump, biography, vague traits |
| Key achievements | top 4 to 6 reusable proof bullets | flagship scope, impact, reliability, AI/governance proof, transformation range | filler bullets, role responsibilities |
| Core competencies | ATS-visible grouped terms | only evidence-backed capabilities allowed for competency surface | every tool ever touched, unsupported nice-to-haves |
| Experience | chronological proof | role summaries, scoped bullets, concrete systems, outcomes | generic responsibilities, duplicate competency dumps |
| Selected projects | optional concentrated proof | flagship systems that help cross-role tailoring | project graveyard |
| Education/certs/languages | supporting metadata | only true, concise items | decorative detail |

### 4.3 Family-specific emphasis

| Lane | Section to expand | Section to compress |
|---|---|---|
| Architect | Header proof line, summary, key achievements, projects | Generic management/profile fluff |
| Head-of-AI adjacency | Summary, key achievements, experience, projects | Executive-only language, large management metadata |
| Staff/Principal IC | Key achievements, competencies, experience | Standalone leadership claims without technical proof |
| Senior AI engineer | Experience and competencies | Overly abstract architecture framing |
| Tech lead AI | Summary, competencies, experience | Executive or strategy-only blocks |
| AI engineering manager | Summary, key achievements, experience | Product/budget/exec claims without evidence |

## 5. Header Skeleton And Metadata Display Policy

### 5.1 ATS-safe default skeleton

```text
NAME
CHOSEN TITLE
Location | Phone | Email | LinkedIn | GitHub/Portfolio
Proof line (optional but preferred when 4.2.2 says proof_dense)
Differentiator line (optional)
```

Rules:

- Keep all contact information in the main body, not in a PDF header/footer.
- Keep the title line literal and scan-friendly.
- Use one metadata line, not a visual sidebar.
- Proof line should be a short proof spine, not a sentence fragment soup.

### 5.2 Recruiter-rich skeleton

```text
NAME
CHOSEN TITLE
Location | Phone | Email | LinkedIn | GitHub/Portfolio
Identity/tagline line
Proof line
Differentiator line
```

Use this when:

- the application is recruiter-forward or email-forward;
- the market tolerates a richer top block;
- the added line density does not hurt ATS parseability.

### 5.3 GCC-enriched skeleton

```text
NAME
CHOSEN TITLE
Current location | Phone | Email | LinkedIn
Nationality | Visa status | Languages | Driving license (if useful)
Proof line
```

Use only when the display policy explicitly allows it and the target market
benefits from it.

### 5.4 Metadata policy matrix

| Field | ATS-safe default | EEA / DACH | UK / global default | UAE / KSA / GCC | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Location | show | show | show | show | city/country is nearly always useful |
| Phone / email / LinkedIn | show | show | show | show | always top-line |
| GitHub / portfolio | show when relevant | show when relevant | show when relevant | show when relevant | only if it helps the role |
| Languages | section by default | section; optionally header when job requires | section; optionally header when job requires | header or section is acceptable | do not crowd the main title line |
| Work authorization / visa | omit unless hiring friction is material | can show for relocation/eligibility-sensitive roles | show only when directly useful | often show in header metadata line | prefer "Work authorization: EU/Germany" over vague claims |
| Relocation / remote posture | optional metadata line | optional | optional | optional and often useful | good for remote/global/GCC friction reduction |
| Nationality | omit by default | optional and candidate-controlled; not required | omit | often expected and useful | store once, reveal by display policy |
| Date of birth | omit by default | optional and candidate-controlled only | omit | market-optional; never default ATS-safe | never make this global default |
| Photo | omit | optional in local German-style variants | omit | more common, especially customer-facing roles | never in ATS-safe variant |
| Marital status | omit | omit | omit | only if explicitly requested and candidate-approved | not part of the truthful default CV |
| Driving license | omit unless role-relevant | optional if role-relevant | optional if role-relevant | often useful when region/job values mobility | keep role-specific |

External validation behind this matrix:

- UK National Careers Service explicitly says not to include age, date of
  birth, marital status, or nationality on the CV.
- Bundesagentur guidance still treats date of birth as a standard personal
  detail in Germany, while making family details and nationality voluntary and
  photo optional.
- GulfTalent's Dubai guidance still treats nationality, age/date of birth,
  visa status, and sometimes photo as common local CV elements.

The implication for 4.3.1 is simple:

- store all candidate-approved personal facts centrally;
- never make sensitive fields part of the default public surface;
- let `display_policy` decide variant-by-variant disclosure.

### 5.5 Title, tagline, and identity-statement rules

Chosen title:

- must come from `role_metadata.acceptable_titles.*`;
- must honor 4.2.2 `title_strategy`;
- must never climb into executive framing without direct truth.

Tagline:

- one line, usually 8 to 18 words;
- should signal identity plus proof, not aspiration;
- should read like a hiring thesis, not marketing copy.

Recommended formula:

```text
<identity family> for <scope/system type> with <proof category>
```

Examples of structure, not literal copy:

- `Architect for enterprise AI platforms with delivery, governance, and reliability proof`
- `Hands-on technical lead for Python and AI systems with mentoring and production-readiness depth`

Identity statement:

- belongs in the summary, not as a separate essay;
- first sentence should answer "who is this candidate on paper for this job?"
- should name seniority and operating mode before tools.

Recommended formula:

```text
<seniority/identity> who <designs/builds/leads> <system scope> and proves <top 2 proof categories>.
```

## 6. Bullet Point And Competency Methodology

### 6.1 Bullet budgets

- Summary: 2 to 4 sentences.
- Key achievements: 4 to 6 bullets.
- Current role: 5 to 6 bullets.
- Previous major role: 3 to 5 bullets.
- Older roles: 1 to 3 bullets.
- Core competencies: 8 to 12 grouped terms, maximum 14.

### 6.2 Bullet anatomy

Every strong bullet should contain three parts:

1. what you changed or built;
2. the scope, constraint, or system context;
3. the outcome, quality gate, or measurable proof.

Recommended shape:

```text
<Action + system/initiative> for <scope/constraint>, resulting in <outcome/proof>.
```

Examples of good bullet behavior:

- architecture decision tied to shipped system;
- governance tied to production control;
- mentoring tied to technical quality or delivery outcome;
- modernization tied to reliability or scale.

Examples of bad bullet behavior:

- responsibility-only bullets;
- naked tool lists;
- vague impact verbs with no proof;
- leadership claims with no evidence of what changed.

### 6.3 Competency rules

Only surface a skill in `core_competencies` when at least one of the
following is true:

- it appears in multiple roles/projects;
- it is central to a flagship achievement;
- it is a repeated market keyword in the target lane and clearly evidenced;
- it is authorized for competency surface in the master-CV sidecar.

Do not surface a skill in `core_competencies` when:

- it appears only once in passing;
- it is inferred from adjacent work but never stated;
- it would be true only in a narrower sense than the heading implies;
- it was flagged as unsafe in eval baselines or scorecards.

### 6.4 Key-achievement rules

Key achievements should be reusable, category-safe, and stronger than normal
experience bullets. They should prioritize:

- flagship platform/system ownership;
- delivery + reliability outcomes;
- AI governance/evaluation/control proof;
- measurable modernization or scale improvement;
- player-coach or stakeholder translation only when tied to delivery.

## 7. ATS-Safe Rules

The current repo guidance and external validation converge on the same rules:

- single-column layout only for ATS-safe exports;
- standard section names only (`Summary`, `Experience`, `Education`,
  `Skills`/`Core Competencies`, `Certifications`, `Languages`);
- no tables, sidebars, text boxes, charts, or image-based skill bars;
- no critical content in PDF headers or footers;
- dates should be explicit and parser-safe;
- exact title match is helpful when truthful;
- acronym plus expansion is better than acronym alone when relevant;
- skills lists should be plain text separated by bullets, bars, or commas;
- the top third must carry title, identity, and at least one proof hook.

For same-company multi-role tenure, use umbrella formatting instead of visual
columns.

## 8. Manual 4.3.1 Review Workflow

### 8.1 Review order

1. Review `candidate_facts.yml`.
2. Review global `role_metadata.json`.
3. Review every role markdown file and create or update its sidecar.
4. Review every project markdown file and create or update its sidecar.
5. Review taxonomies and only add ids that are candidate-proven.
6. Run deterministic validation and freeze the checksum boundary.

Do not start with prose polishing. Start with truth inventory.

### 8.2 File-by-file responsibilities

| File | Manual decisions required |
|---|---|
| `data/master-cv/candidate_facts.yml` | contact, location, languages, education, certifications, work authorization, sensitive personal facts, display policy |
| `data/master-cv/role_metadata.json` | identity summary, acceptable titles, not-identity tags, global differentiators, global credibility markers |
| `data/master-cv/roles/*.md` | source narrative, role summary, bullet order, explicit system names, metrics, stakeholder context |
| `data/master-cv/roles/*.meta.json` | scope bands, seniority, stakeholder exposure, AI relevance, skill evidence, achievement surface authorization |
| `data/master-cv/projects/*.md` | concise project descriptions and ordered project bullets |
| `data/master-cv/projects/*.meta.json` | ownership, architecture, reliability, AI relevance, business impact, cross-links to role achievements |
| `data/master-cv/taxonomies/*.json` | only add ids that can be cited by current evidence |

### 8.3 Surface authorization model

Every reusable fact, skill, and achievement should be classified into one or
more surfaces:

| Surface | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `header_proof` | safe for proof line or differentiator line |
| `summary_safe` | safe for summary prose |
| `key_achievement` | strong enough for top achievement block |
| `core_competency` | safe to appear as a competency keyword |
| `experience_only` | true, but only defensible in role/project detail |
| `regional_optional` | safe only in region-specific variants |
| `recruiter_private` | useful in recruiter conversations or application forms, not default CV body |
| `do_not_use` | true but too weak, too sensitive, or too misleading for CV output |

This classification is the easiest manual way to stop the exact failure mode
seen in eval: unsupported skills or metadata leaking into high-visibility
surfaces.

### 8.4 `candidate_facts.yml` review checklist

For each field, decide:

1. Is it true?
2. Is it current?
3. Does it need evidence or a confidence note?
4. Is it public by default, recruiter-only, region-specific, or private?

Required manual decisions:

- `location_primary`
- `languages[]` and proficiency
- `work_authorization[]`
- `remote_posture`
- `relocation_posture`
- `display_policy.default_variant`
- `display_policy.region_overrides[]`

Sensitive fields such as nationality or date of birth should be stored only
if candidate-approved and should default to non-public unless a region policy
explicitly permits them.

### 8.5 `role_metadata.json` review checklist

Decide the candidate-wide identity envelope:

- primary identity tags;
- allowed title families by strategy band (`exact`, `closest_truthful`,
  `functional_label`);
- disallowed identities;
- candidate-wide differentiators;
- global credibility markers.

This file should encode the lanes the eval corpus says are safe:

- architect-first,
- player-coach technical lead,
- senior IC / staff / principal,
- conservative AI engineering manager only where evidence supports it.

It should not encode:

- executive Head of AI identity as the default;
- literal people-manager identity without strong evidence;
- provider-specific authority without direct delivery proof.

### 8.6 Per-role review checklist

For each role:

1. Re-read the role markdown.
2. Mark explicit systems, outcomes, stakeholder settings, and scope markers.
3. Fill role sidecar fields only when supported.
4. Leave unsupported numeric fields `null`.
5. Classify every major achievement by:
   - proof categories,
   - experience dimensions,
   - AI relevance band,
   - scope band,
   - metric band,
   - allowed surfaces.
6. Map skills to evidence and to safe surfaces.
7. Mark unresolved fields explicitly.

Priority questions per role:

- Was this role architect-first, builder-first, tech-lead-first, or
  manager-first in reality?
- What systems can be named safely?
- Which outcomes are flagship enough for header or key-achievement use?
- Which facts are true but only safe inside detailed experience?
- Which skills should never appear in competencies?

### 8.7 Per-project review checklist

For each project:

1. tighten the one-line description so it reads like a system, not a hobby;
2. order bullets by market value, not chronology;
3. capture ownership level;
4. capture architecture and reliability markers;
5. capture AI relevance, governance, evaluation, retrieval, and integration
   only if directly supported;
6. cross-link back to role achievements.

Project bullet order matters because the eval process already showed that
high-visibility bullets materially shape perceived fit.

### 8.8 Taxonomy review checklist

Only add taxonomy entries when:

- the system, domain, identity, or leadership marker is genuinely part of the
  candidate record;
- at least one role/project can cite it;
- you are comfortable defending it in interview language.

Do not use taxonomy growth as a way to "prepare" future claims.

### 8.9 Definition of done for one role

A role is manually complete when:

- the markdown is ordered and readable;
- sidecar fields reflect only supported truth;
- every flagship achievement has proof categories and dimensions;
- high-visibility achievements are marked for safe surfaces;
- unsupported skills are not competency-authorized;
- unresolved fields are explicit;
- the review stamp records manual review.

## 9. Downstream Implications

### 9.1 4.3.2 Header blueprint

Consumes:

- global title allowlist,
- identity tags,
- candidate display policy,
- per-achievement surface authorization.

Must output:

- one truthful chosen title,
- one default header surface policy,
- one ATS-safe path that always works,
- optional richer regional variants when allowed.

### 9.2 4.3.3 Pattern selection

Should use eval-derived lane priors:

- architect categories -> architecture/platform/delivery patterns dominate;
- head-of-AI categories -> architecture plus delivery, not executive-only;
- staff/senior categories -> technical and reliability-heavy patterns;
- tech-lead categories -> delivery plus mentoring/player-coach;
- AI engineering manager -> player-coach delivery manager, not executive.

### 9.3 4.3.4 Draft assembly

Must respect:

- surface authorization,
- competency authorization,
- ATS-safe section ordering,
- bullet budgets,
- region/display variant decisions.

### 9.4 4.3.5 Grading and synthesis

Must penalize:

- unsupported competency inflation,
- sensitive metadata leakage,
- mismatch between lane/persona and chosen title,
- ATS-hostile structure,
- executive drift.

### 9.5 4.3.6 Rendering and publishing

Should support at least three output modes:

- `ats_safe`
- `recruiter_rich`
- `regional_enriched`

The data model should store facts once and render them differently by policy,
not rewrite the truth per market.
