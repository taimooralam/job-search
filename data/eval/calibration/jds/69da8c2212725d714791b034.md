# Data & AI Director
**Company:** Confidential
**Location:** Not specified

## Responsibilities
- Sets Authority-wide data governance framework, roles, and standards for quality, protection, retention, metadata, and lineage
- Directs design and delivery of analytical products and AI/ML models supporting registration, filing, revenue, compliance risk, and service operations
- Oversees data platforms architecture and roadmap for ingestion, integration, storage, and processing
- Maintains data/AI risk register and defines controls for privacy, model risk, explainability, and compliance
- Defines KPIs and dashboards for data quality, platform reliability, model performance, and BI adoption
- Coordinates with IT, Applications, Infrastructure, and business departments on integrations, security, and releases
- Ensures data privacy and access controls with Cybersecurity and Enterprise Risk teams
- Leads experimentation, PoCs, and model lifecycle reviews; retires and refreshes models based on performance
- Manages vendor contracts, tools, licenses, and tracks run-vs-change spend and unit costs
- Builds data literacy and self-service capabilities through standards, training, and governed semantic layer

## Qualifications
- Bachelor's degree in Data Science, Computer Science, Information Systems, Statistics, Engineering, Economics, or related field
- 16+ years across data governance/management, analytics/AI, and data platform engineering
- Proven leadership of multi-disciplinary teams
- Delivered organization-wide data standards, high-value analytical products, and resilient data platforms
- Experience embedding models into operations with risk scoring, anomaly detection, and monitoring outcomes
- Coordination with cybersecurity, applications, infrastructure, and business departments
- Vendor and contract management experience

## Implied Pain Points
- Fragmented data governance with inconsistent standards across business units
- Poor data quality impacting operational decisions and compliance reporting
- Lack of embedded analytics in operational workflows and case management
- Siloed data platforms requiring integration and modernization
- Compliance and privacy risks from uncontrolled data access and usage
- Low data literacy preventing self-service analytics adoption
- Aging or underperforming AI/ML models requiring lifecycle management and refresh
- Unclear ROI measurement and business value tracking for data/AI investments

## Success Metrics
- Improved data quality scores for critical data elements with sustained remediation
- Increased adoption of self-service BI dashboards and analytical products
- Successful deployment of AI models into production with measurable operational impact
- Reduced data governance incidents and closed audit findings with sustainable fixes
- Platform uptime, latency, and performance SLAs consistently met
- Optimized costs for data platforms, tools, and vendor contracts
- Measurable business outcomes from analytical products supporting policy and compliance
- Enhanced data literacy and self-service capabilities across the organization
