## Key Achievement Archetypes
### 1. Architected and shipped a production AI platform or gateway
- What it proves: Shows the core category value: architecture ownership, reusable platform thinking, and production delivery under reliability constraints.
- Pain points addressed: need to define and drive ai technical strategy, need to build and scale advanced ai systems in production, need to establish ml engineering standards, security, and governance
- Metrics to include: time to production, adoption by teams or users, supported workflows or integrations, reliability or availability, latency or throughput, cost per request or routing efficiency
- Story format guidance: Use ARIS: state the architecture problem, your platform design, the implementation choices, and measurable production outcomes. Make the reusable-system boundary explicit, not just a single feature shipped.
- Citation: Platform design 24/25 (96%); valued evidence types architecture 20 and delivery 18 deep-analysis samples; agents_orchestration 12/25 (48%).

### 2. Took an agent or RAG system from POC to reliable production
- What it proves: Shows you can move beyond demos into grounded, observable, production-safe AI systems.
- Pain points addressed: need to choose appropriate solutions through pocs, need to build and scale advanced ai systems in production, need to modernize and improve code quality while integrating ai services
- Metrics to include: evaluation pass rate, hallucination or error reduction, retrieval quality or answer accuracy, workflow completion rate, user adoption, deployment frequency
- Story format guidance: Use STAR: frame the weak prototype or business need, show the architecture and evaluation changes you led, then quantify production quality and adoption. Name guardrails and validation steps if they were part of the delivery.
- Citation: AI stack signals: agents_orchestration 12/25 (48%), RAG 9/25 (36%), evaluation_quality 5/25 (20%), vector_search 5/25 (20%); success metrics include handling hallucinations (1) and structured output validation (1).

### 3. Modernized or integrated complex enterprise systems around AI or cloud services

- Highest-weight roles: Staff/Principal IC roles with architecture ownership for production platforms or distributed systems., Hands-on AI platform, backend platform, or developer tooling roles that shipped real systems., Player-coach roles with mentoring or standards-setting, but limited formal management scope.
