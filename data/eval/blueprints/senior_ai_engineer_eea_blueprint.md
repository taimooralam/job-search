## Key Achievement Archetypes
### 1. Shipped a production RAG or agent workflow into a real product or internal platform
- What it proves: Proves the strongest combined signal in the category: hands-on architecture plus applied GenAI delivery with production accountability.
- Pain points addressed: deploy production RAG systems and AI agents, build scalable AI-driven products and experiences, unlock GenAI across products or internal teams
- Metrics to include: active users or teams served, task completion or automation rate, latency or response-time change, quality or acceptance-rate improvement, number of integrations, tools, or plugins, uptime or incident rate
- Story format guidance: Use STAR or ARIS. Start with the product or workflow constraint, then show the architecture you chose, what you shipped, and the measurable production result.
- Citation: platform_design 7/7 (100.0%); RAG 5/7 (71.4%); agents_orchestration 5/7 (71.4%); delivery/reliability/scale valued in 7/7 deep analyses

### 2. Integrated AI capabilities into an existing product, service stack, or workflow instead of building a standalone demo
- What it proves: Proves system integration skill, legacy-aware engineering, and the ability to make AI useful in real operating environments.
- Pain points addressed: integrate AI solutions into existing environments and workflows, integrate models into existing products, build and maintain internal ML operation services
- Metrics to include: time to integrate or launch, services or teams integrated, adoption or usage growth, defect rate or rollback rate, throughput or SLA impact
- Story format guidance: Frame the before-state clearly: existing system constraints, interfaces, or reliability requirements. Then show how the AI capability was embedded and what changed for users or operators.
- Citation: greenfield_split both 4/7 (57.1%); platform_design 7/7 (100.0%); pain points on integration/product embedding each appear 1/7 in a sparse sample

### 3. Built evaluation, testing, or quality controls that improved trust and production readiness
- What it proves: Proves that the candidate can move beyond feature shipping into measurable quality control, which is highly valued in this market slice.
- Pain points addressed: maintain high quality bars, ensure seamless operation and performance optimization, test and deploy software solutions that meet business needs
- Metrics to include: evaluation pass rate, hallucination or error reduction, manual review reduction, release confidence or deployment frequency, incident reduction or MTTR
- Story format guidance: Use ARIS. Show the quality risk first, then the evaluation or testing mechanism introduced, then the measurable reduction in defects, regressions, or review load.
- Citation: evaluation valued in 6/7 deep analyses; evaluation_quality 4/7 (57.1%); reliability valued in 7/7

### 4. Designed architecture that improved scale, resilience, or operational efficiency for AI-enabled systems
