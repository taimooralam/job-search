## Tagline and Profile Angle
Positioning angles:
- Position as the person who turns AI concepts into production architecture, integration patterns, and reliable delivery: platform design 36/38 (94.7%), delivery 20/21 (95.2%), reliability 17/21 (81.0%).
- Position as a hands-on architect or player-coach who can mentor and translate across teams without overstating org ownership: mentoring 18/38 (47.4%), collaboration 31/38 (81.6%), communication 26/38 (68.4%), hiring 2/38 (5.3%).
Foreground:
- Architecture blueprints and system design for production AI platforms.
- Delivery outcomes: shipped systems, scaled services, improved quality, shortened cycle times.
- Reliability, observability, evaluation, and guardrails in production settings.
- Cross-functional translation across engineering, product, and enterprise stakeholders.
Avoid:
- Executive-org language that implies headcount, budget, or formal business ownership.
- Research-centered framing, publication emphasis, or model-scientist positioning.
- Claims of owning end-to-end MLOps, vector database architecture, or custom LLM training unless explicitly evidenced.
- Region-specific compliance or domain authority claims unless repeated and provable.
Safe positioning:
- Production AI architect with delivery and reliability evidence.
- Hands-on technical lead for AI integrations, orchestration, and governance.
- Player-coach mentor who improves system quality and team execution without inflating people-management scope.
Unsafe positioning:
- AI executive / business owner with budget or P&L accountability.
- Research-led ML scientist or custom-model lead.
- Formal people manager with a sizeable reporting line.
- Regulated-industry AI governance lead without explicit compliance ownership.
Citations:
- Soft skills: collaboration 31/38 (81.6%), communication 26/38 (68.4%), mentoring 18/38 (47.4%).
- Management evidence remains limited: hiring 2/38 (5.3%), performance management 3/38 (7.9%), org_building 4/38 (10.5%), budget_pnl 0/38 (0.0%).
- Unsafe claims in deep analysis include budget/P&L, custom LLM training, mechanistic interpretability, patents/publications, full MLOps ownership, vector database architecture, and formal people management; each appears only 1-2 times across 21 deep analyses.

## Key Achievement Archetypes
### 1. Architecture blueprint turned into production AI platform
- What it proves: Proves category fit on the strongest market signal: architecture ownership tied to real delivery, scalability, and production adoption.
- Pain points addressed: deliver value quickly in a high-growth global environment, improve performance, quality, and communication across departments
- Metrics to include: systems or services launched, adoption or tenant count, latency / throughput / uptime, deployment frequency, time-to-production
- Story format guidance: Start with the platform constraint, name the architecture decision, then quantify delivery and scale outcomes. End with how the design improved production readiness or cross-team execution.
- Citation: Platform design 36/38 (94.7%); architecture evidence 21/21 (100.0%); delivery evidence 20/21 (95.2%); scale evidence 16/21 (76.2%).

### 2. Reliability and observability uplift for AI or integration workflows
- What it proves: Proves the candidate can productionize AI systems beyond demos by improving resilience, traceability, and operational confidence.
- Pain points addressed: eliminate obstacles for a remote engineering team, code review devolves into perfunctory approval
- Metrics to include: incident rate, MTTR, SLO attainment, error budget burn, alert volume, debug time
- Story format guidance: Use an ARIS structure: baseline instability, intervention in observability or reliability design, measurable service improvement, then resulting team efficiency. Keep it operational and quantified.
- Citation: Reliability evidence 17/21 (81.0%); observability 6/38 (15.8%); Datadog 3/38 (7.9%).

### 3. Evaluation and guardrails implementation for trustworthy outputs
- What it proves: Proves applied AI maturity by showing the candidate can raise quality, reduce risk, and make outputs more dependable in production environments.
- Pain points addressed: transparent and trustworthy recommendations for security professionals, production-ready genai solutions
- Metrics to include: quality score improvement, hallucination or error reduction, acceptance rate, manual review reduction, policy violation rate
- Story format guidance: Frame the problem as output quality or trust risk, specify the evaluation or guardrail mechanism, then quantify the gain in acceptance, quality, or safety. Avoid policy-leadership claims unless they were formal responsibilities.
- Citation: Evaluation evidence 11/21 (52.4%); evaluation-quality 8/38 (21.1%); guardrails/governance 5/38 (13.2%); governance evidence 10/21 (47.6%).

### 4. Partner or enterprise integration architecture across distributed systems
- What it proves: Proves the candidate can connect AI or platform capabilities into real business environments, especially across partner APIs, enterprise constraints, and cross-team dependencies.
- Pain points addressed: integrate with global partner apis, deliver value quickly in a high-growth global environment
- Metrics to include: integration count, partner onboarding time, failure rate reduction, throughput, customer or internal-user adoption
- Story format guidance: Describe the enterprise integration challenge, the routing or orchestration design, and the measured effect on delivery speed, reliability, or usability. Keep the emphasis on system integration, not speculative AI novelty.
- Citation: Pain point: integrate with global partner APIs (2 mentions); consulting 5/38 (13.2%), enterprise SaaS 4/38 (10.5%), cybersecurity 3/38 (7.9%), fintech 3/38 (7.9%).

### 5. Delivery acceleration through process, automation, and mentoring
- What it proves: Proves player-coach value: the candidate improves team output and engineering quality without needing inflated org-builder claims.
- Pain points addressed: increase team productivity through process optimization, automation, and efficiency improvements, backlogs expand, cycle times stretch across weeks
- Metrics to include: cycle time, lead time, PR turnaround, defect escape rate, team throughput, automation coverage
- Story format guidance: Show the execution bottleneck, the process or automation change, and the quantified throughput or quality gain. Add mentoring or design-review influence only if it directly contributed to the result.
- Citation: Delivery evidence 20/21 (95.2%); mentoring 18/38 (47.4%); pain points on productivity, backlog growth, and cycle time each appear in deep analysis.

## Role Weighting Guidance
- Highest-weight roles: AI / Platform Architect, Principal or Staff Engineer with AI platform ownership, Technical Lead / Player-Coach for AI systems and integrations
Expand in work history:
- Roles where you owned architecture decisions, platform design, or enterprise integration patterns.
- Roles where AI-related systems reached production and you can quantify delivery, reliability, evaluation, or governance outcomes.
- Roles where you mentored engineers, led design reviews, or translated across engineering, product, and stakeholder groups.
- Remote or distributed delivery roles when you improved execution or communication in those environments.
Compress in work history:
- Pure people-management roles with limited technical ownership.
- Research-only work, publication-style framing, or model-science claims that did not drive shipped systems.
- Older roles that do not support distributed systems, integration, cloud, or architecture credibility.
- Domain claims that are weakly evidenced, such as formal regulated-environment governance leadership.
How to frame non-AI experience:
- Translate distributed systems, microservices, cloud, API integration, and observability work into the foundations of AI productionization.
- Map quality, testing, governance, or reliability work from non-AI systems into production AI readiness rather than pretending the work was AI-native.
- Use AI-specific terminology only where the work truly involved AI features, AI platforms, RAG, orchestration, evaluation, or guardrails.
