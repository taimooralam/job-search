## Category Signature
An architecture-led enterprise AI leadership category where platform design and production delivery dominate, people leadership is present but not universal, and governed agentic AI adoption matters more than pure executive branding or niche framework name-dropping.

Distinctive signals:
- Architecture outweighs pure executive scope: solutions_architect titles were 7/16 (43.8%), engineering_manager 5/16 (31.2%), while executive roles were 2/16 (12.5%), and platform design appeared in 87.5% of the sample.
- Management is real but moderate: leadership was 10/16 (62.5%), mentoring 8/16 (50.0%), org building 7/16 (43.8%), performance management 6/16 (37.5%), hiring 4/16 (25.0%), and budget/P&L only 2/16 (12.5%).

## Key Achievement Archetypes
### 1. Built or re-architected an AI/platform capability and put it into production
- What it proves: Proves the core fit for this category: architecture leadership tied to delivery, not just ideation or title inflation.
- Pain points addressed: build a brand new agentic ai squad, embed agentic ai capabilities into core tools, align engineering objectives and roadmap with business goals
- Metrics to include: time to launch, number of systems integrated, internal or customer users enabled, delivery milestones shipped, latency or throughput change if relevant
- Story format guidance: Use ARIS: name the architecture or platform problem, the integration/rollout actions you owned, and the production outcome. Show design decisions and operating results, not only feature lists.
- Citation: Platform design 87.5%; delivery valued in 16/16 deep analyses; architecture 14/16; pain points for new agentic AI squad count=2 and embedding AI into core tools count=2.

### 2. Improved reliability, observability, or maintainability of a SaaS or internal platform
- What it proves: Proves operating maturity and makes the CV materially different from generic GenAI leadership profiles.
- Pain points addressed: keep the saas product performant, reliable, and maintainable, improving platform reliability and observability
- Metrics to include: availability or SLO improvement, incident reduction, MTTR reduction, performance or latency improvement, maintenance load reduction
- Story format guidance: Use STAR: define the service-health problem, the reliability levers you changed, and the before/after operating metrics. Make the metric line explicit.
- Citation: Reliability valued in 8/16 deep analyses, observability 3/16 (18.8%), performance success metric count=2, platform health count=2, pain points on reliability/observability count=2 each.

### 3. Embedded governed AI workflows with evaluation or control points
- What it proves: Proves practical enterprise AI adoption rather than shallow framework familiarity.
- Pain points addressed: embed agentic ai capabilities into core tools, align engineering objectives and roadmap with business goals
- Metrics to include: evaluation pass rate or quality score, adoption or active users, workflow cycle-time reduction, risk/control coverage, support ticket reduction
- Story format guidance: Use ARIS: show the workflow or business process, the governance/evaluation controls added, and the measurable adoption or quality outcome. Keep model/framework names secondary to delivery proof.
- Citation: Agents_orchestration 2/16 (12.5%), evaluation_quality 2/16 (12.5%), guardrails_governance 2/16 (12.5%), governance valued in 8/16 deep analyses, evaluation in 5/16.

### 4. Led cross-functional roadmap execution from business goal to shipped outcome
- What it proves: Proves translation ability between executives, product, architecture, and engineering teams.
- Pain points addressed: align engineering objectives and roadmap with business goals, scale and manage a cross-functional engineering organization
- Metrics to include: roadmap delivery rate, time-to-delivery, stakeholder satisfaction proxy, cross-team dependency reduction, business KPI moved if directly attributable
- Story format guidance: Use STAR: state the business goal first, then the technical coordination challenge, then the shipped outcome with dates or metrics. Avoid vague 'partnered with stakeholders' phrasing without results.
- Citation: Collaboration 13/16 (81.2%), communication 9/16 (56.2%), stakeholder management 6/16 (37.5%), pain points on roadmap alignment count=2 and cross-functional engineering organization count=2.

### 5. Developed team capability while staying close to architecture or delivery
- What it proves: Proves player-coach leadership without overstating pure executive ownership.
- Pain points addressed: scale and manage a cross-functional engineering organization, build a brand new agentic ai squad
- Metrics to include: team size or span if true, retention or promotion outcomes, hiring count if true, delivery throughput improvement, mentor/coaching outcomes
- Story format guidance: Use ARIS: define the team capability gap, show the coaching/performance actions you personally drove, and quantify delivery or people outcomes. Only include hiring or org design if directly evidenced.
- Citation: Leadership 10/16 (62.5%), mentoring 8/16 (50.0%), performance_management 6/16 (37.5%), org_building 7/16 (43.8%), hiring 4/16 (25.0%).
