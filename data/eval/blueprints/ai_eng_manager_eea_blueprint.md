## Category Signature
Position this category as a player-coach AI engineering leader who ships and scales AI platform or product capabilities, translates between stakeholders and engineering, and proves quality, governance, and adoption without overstating formal product-management or org-ownership claims.

Distinctive signals:
- The real archetype is delivery-led manager or player-coach, not executive: manager scope appears in 14/31 roles (45.2%), IC scope in 11/31 (35.5%), player-coach in 5/31 (16.1%), and executive in 1/31 (3.2%).

## Key Achievement Archetypes
### 1. Shipped AI platform or workflow capability with measurable adoption
- What it proves: Proves the core category fit: delivery, scale, user value, and end-to-end execution of AI capabilities.
- Pain points addressed: prototype, test, and deploy ai product capabilities, drive adoption and end-to-end product outcomes, driving ai product launches from concept to launch
- Metrics to include: active users or internal adopters, usage growth or adoption rate, time to launch or time to value, workflow volume, plugin count, or integration count, revenue influence, productivity gain, or cost avoided
- Story format guidance: Use STAR or ARIS with the product or platform problem first, the shipped AI capability second, and measurable adoption or value third. Keep the action centered on delivery and iteration rather than abstract strategy.
- Citation: Valued evidence: delivery 17/17, scale 17/17; success metrics measurable value 10/17, delivery 10/17, user impact 10/17, adoption 6/17.

### 2. Designed platform architecture that made AI delivery production-ready
- What it proves: Proves the candidate can move beyond demos into stable AI systems through architecture, integration, and productionization.
- Pain points addressed: prototype, test, and deploy ai product capabilities, align priorities with engineering, design, leadership, and executives
- Metrics to include: latency or throughput improvement, provider or model integration count, reliability or incident reduction, deployment frequency or lead time, cost per request or infrastructure efficiency

- Citation: Architecture: platform design 17/31 (54.8%); valued evidence type architecture 11/17; greenfield split both 19 and greenfield 4.

### 3. Improved AI quality, trust, and governance so the system could scale safely
