# Head of AI — UAE — Eval Rubric

## Meta
- Category ID: head_of_ai_uae
- Macro family: ai_leadership
- Priority: primary_target
- Confidence: high
- Rubric version: 2026-04-17

## Identity
- Purpose: Evaluate CVs for architect-first AI platform leadership roles in UAE that reward production delivery, governance, reliability, and stakeholder translation over executive org-building or advanced research claims.
- Core persona: Architect-first player-coach who translates enterprise AI and platform requirements into production-ready systems with governance, reliability, and operational readiness; technical leadership through delivery influence rather than formal executive authority.
Job overlay notes:
- Title noise is high in this category; Solutions Architect and AI Architect titles dominate over literal Head of AI executive labels.
- IC and player-coach scope represents 85% of roles; formal people-management, org-building, and budget ownership are weak signals.
- Production AI delivery with guardrails and evaluation readiness differentiates stronger candidates more than framework inventories.
- Multi-cloud architecture judgment is valued; single-vendor cloud branding should be delivery-backed rather than identity-led.
- Stakeholder translation and customer-facing advisory are table-stakes soft skills for this market.

## Dimension Rubrics
### ats_optimization (weight 20)
**Layer A — Category core**
What good looks like:
- Headline leads with architect-family title such as Solutions Architect, AI Architect, or Enterprise Architect rather than inflated executive labels.
- Keywords include solutions architecture, enterprise architecture, platform design, production readiness, reliability, governance, and multi-cloud with 2-3 mentions each where true.
- Technical keywords are distributed across headline, profile, experience, and skills sections without keyword stuffing.
- Cloud names appear 1-2 times each only where delivery proof exists; multi-cloud appears as architecture judgment rather than tool familiarity.
Common failures:
- Leading with Head of AI, VP AI, or Director of AI without supporting executive evidence.
- Over-indexing on framework names like LangChain or Semantic Kernel when architecture and delivery signals are stronger.
- Generic AI strategy or transformation language without shipped-system proof.
- Missing core keywords: solutions architecture, platform design, reliability, governance, stakeholder management.
**Layer B — Job-specific overlay**
Adjust per JD:
- If JD emphasizes Solutions Architect title family, lead with that exact phrasing in headline.
- If JD mentions specific cloud provider, include that provider 1-2 times with delivery context.
- If JD emphasizes customer-facing work, foreground stakeholder management and solution design keywords.
- If JD mentions governance or compliance, include guardrails, evaluation, and governance keywords.
Company/domain overrides:
- Enterprise SaaS contexts: emphasize platform design, multi-tenant architecture, and integration patterns.
- Fintech contexts: include reliability, security-aware architecture, and compliance-sensitive language if evidenced.
- Consulting or professional services: foreground customer advisory, solution design, and architecture decision support.
Region overrides:
- UAE market: avoid region-specific regulatory claims unless explicitly evidenced.
- Do not claim Arabic language capability or regional transformation scope without direct proof.
**Score anchors**
- 9-10:
  - Headline uses architect-family title matching the dominant category pattern with enterprise AI, platform design, and reliability keywords.
  - All table-stakes keywords appear naturally 2-3 times across sections with no stuffing or missing coverage.
  - Technical and soft skill keywords align precisely with JD requirements and category blueprint signals.
- 7-8:
  - Headline is credible architect or technical lead framing with most core keywords present.
  - Minor gaps in keyword distribution or slight over-reliance on one section.
  - Cloud and framework keywords are present but could be more precisely delivery-backed.
- 5-6:
  - Headline is generic or slightly misaligned with category title patterns.
  - Several core keywords missing or appearing only once.
  - Some keyword stuffing or unnatural repetition detected.
- <=4:
  - Headline uses inflated executive title without supporting evidence.
  - Major keyword gaps in architecture, platform design, or reliability language.
  - Heavy framework or tool keyword stuffing with weak architecture and delivery signals.
Red flags:
- Leading with Head of AI, VP AI, or Chief AI Officer without executive scope proof.
- Framework-heavy keyword lists without architecture and delivery context.
- Missing solutions architecture, platform design, or reliability keywords entirely.
- Single cloud vendor as headline identity without delivery proof.

### impact_clarity (weight 25)
**Layer A — Category core**
What good looks like:
- Achievement bullets use STAR or ARIS format with clear before-state, intervention, and quantified outcome.
- Metrics include production-relevant signals: availability, incident reduction, latency, adoption, deployment frequency, onboarding time.
- Platform scale is evidenced through users, tenants, integrations, or throughput rather than team size alone.
- Modernization stories show complexity reduction, reliability improvement, and operational readiness gains.
Common failures:
- Vague responsibility statements without quantified outcomes.
- Metrics focused on team size or project count rather than system impact.
- Missing before-state context that would prove transformation scope.
- AI achievements described as demos or experiments rather than production systems.
**Layer B — Job-specific overlay**
Adjust per JD:
- If JD emphasizes scale, foreground user counts, throughput, and availability metrics.
- If JD emphasizes modernization, lead with incident reduction, deployment frequency, and architectural complexity metrics.
- If JD emphasizes customer success, include customer-facing metrics like satisfaction, retention, or expansion influence.
- If JD emphasizes AI governance, include evaluation coverage, guardrail effectiveness, or quality improvement metrics.
Company/domain overrides:
- Enterprise contexts: emphasize multi-tenant scale, integration count, and cross-team adoption.
- Startup or growth contexts: emphasize velocity improvements, time-to-launch, and growth runway architecture.
- Consulting contexts: emphasize deals influenced, accounts covered, or customer outcomes delivered.
Region overrides:
- UAE enterprise market: reliability and operational readiness metrics are particularly valued.
- Government or regulated contexts: compliance-sensitive metrics only if explicitly evidenced.
**Score anchors**
- 9-10:
  - Every major achievement has quantified outcome with clear before/after context.
  - Metrics span reliability, scale, velocity, and adoption dimensions relevant to platform architecture.
  - AI achievements include production signals: users, uptime, evaluation coverage, guardrail effectiveness.
- 7-8:
  - Most achievements are quantified with reasonable before/after context.
  - Some metrics could be more production-relevant or better contextualized.
  - AI achievements show production deployment but metrics could be sharper.
- 5-6:
  - Achievements are partially quantified but lack consistent before/after framing.
  - Metrics skew toward activity counts rather than outcome measures.
  - AI work is described but production impact is unclear.
- <=4:
  - Achievements are responsibility descriptions without quantified outcomes.
  - Metrics are missing, vague, or focused on irrelevant dimensions.
  - AI work appears experimental or demo-stage without production proof.
Red flags:
- Round-number metrics without supporting context suggesting fabrication.
- Team size as primary impact metric in architect-first category.
- AI achievements described only as POCs, experiments, or research without production deployment.
- Missing reliability, availability, or operational readiness metrics entirely.

## Scoring Guidance
- Executive presence: For this architect-first player-coach-safe category, interpret executive_presence as technical leadership through architecture decisions, standards setting, mentoring, and stakeholder translation. Do NOT require or reward literal executive authority, C-suite access, boardroom presence, or P&L ownership. Penalize unsupported people-management framing when evidence shows IC or player-coach scope. Only credit stronger management framing when baseline evidence explicitly supports hiring, org-building, or budget accountability.
- Unsupported claims: Apply direct score penalties for: unsourced metrics (especially round numbers without context), title inflation beyond actual held positions, PhD or publication emphasis in this 0% PhD-required category, enterprise-wide ownership claims with team-level evidence, compliance program ownership claims beyond implemented controls, and executive scope claims when evidence shows architect or technical lead scope. Each unsupported claim should reduce anti_hallucination score by 1-2 points depending on severity.
- Category vs job tradeoffs: When JD requirements conflict with category blueprint, prioritize category constraints for safety while noting JD-specific gaps. Example: if JD requests Head of AI title but candidate evidence supports architect scope, maintain architect framing and flag the gap rather than inflating. Weight JD-specific technical requirements within category-safe positioning.
