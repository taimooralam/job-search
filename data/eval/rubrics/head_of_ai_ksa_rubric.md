# Head of AI — KSA — Eval Rubric

## Meta
- Category ID: head_of_ai_ksa
- Macro family: ai_leadership
- Priority: primary_target
- Confidence: medium
- Rubric version: 2026-04-17

## Identity
- Purpose: Evaluate CV fit for architecture-led enterprise AI platform leadership roles where platform design and production delivery dominate, people leadership is present but not universal, and governed agentic AI adoption matters more than executive branding or framework name-dropping.
- Core persona: Architecture-led AI platform leader who translates business goals into governed platform execution, maintains player-coach proximity to delivery, and demonstrates reliability and stakeholder translation depth without inflating to executive or country-head scope.
Job overlay notes:
- Architecture proof outweighs title seniority: solutions_architect titles appeared in 43.8% of sample, platform design in 87.5%, while executive scope was only 12.5%.
- People leadership is real but capped: mentoring 50%, performance management 37.5%, but budget/P&L only 12.5% and hiring only 25%.
- AI differentiation must be delivery-led: agents_orchestration 12.5%, evaluation_quality 12.5%, prompt_engineering 0% — delivery evidence appeared in 16/16 deep analyses.
- KSA in category name is target market, not a claim of prior regional remit — do not inflate to country-head without explicit evidence.
- Enterprise and reliability orientation: enterprise company stage 50%, reliability valued in 8/16 deep analyses, governance in 8/16.

### ats_optimization (weight 20)
**Layer A — Category core**

- Primary keywords 'platform architecture', 'solutions architecture', 'AI platform', 'enterprise architecture' appear 2-3 times across headline, profile, and experience when true.
- Secondary keywords 'reliability', 'observability', 'governance', 'evaluation', 'stakeholder alignment' appear 1-2 times each when true.
- Framework names appear once only and only when shipped — no stack dumps or framework-shopping patterns.
- Architecture-led title variants used in headline: 'AI Platform Architect', 'Solutions Architect', 'Enterprise Architect', 'Engineering Manager', 'Technical Lead'.
- Outcome words precede tool names: 'governed AI platform delivery' before 'LangChain/LangGraph'.
Common failures:
- Framework-shopping: listing 'GenAI, LLMs, RAG, agents, LangChain, LangGraph, CrewAI' without shipped architecture proof.
- Prompt-centric positioning when prompt_engineering appeared in 0% of sample.

- Stack dumps in skills section without delivery or operating outcome context.
- Missing architecture keywords when platform design appeared in 87.5% of sample.

- If JD emphasizes team leadership, ensure 'mentoring', 'coaching', 'team development' appear with quantified scope.

Company/domain overrides:
- Enterprise companies: emphasize 'enterprise integration', 'system design', 'cross-system delivery'.

## Scoring Guidance
- Executive presence: For this architect-first player-coach category, executive_presence means player-coach AI platform leadership with stakeholder translation and measured people development — NOT literal executive leadership, C-suite presence, boardroom authority, or P&L ownership. Score high for technical authority combined with team development and collaboration. Score low for executive inflation without multi-team evidence. Red-flag any C-suite, VP, or Chief AI Officer positioning without explicit executive scope proof.
- Unsupported claims: Apply immediate 2-point penalty to anti_hallucination dimension for any unsourced metric, fabricated title, or capability claim exceeding curated evidence. Apply 1-point penalty to executive_presence for any scope inflation (team size, reports, budget) without evidence. Apply 1-point penalty to jd_alignment for any certification or regional claim without provenance. Cumulative penalties can drop overall score below weak_match threshold.
- Category vs job tradeoffs: Category pattern takes precedence: even if a specific JD uses 'Head of AI' title, do not inflate CV to executive positioning unless the candidate has multi-team executive evidence. Adjust keyword emphasis per JD but maintain architecture-led player-coach persona. When JD requirements exceed baseline evidence, note gap in scoring rather than hallucinating capabilities. Prioritize category table stakes over JD nice-to-haves.
