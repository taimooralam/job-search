## Method

Baselines were generated by:
- reading Step 5 blueprints from `data/eval/blueprints/*_blueprint.json`
- comparing them against:
  - curated evidence in `data/master-cv/*`
  - bounded upstream evidence from `docs/archive/knowledge-base.md`, `docs/current/achievement-review-index.md`, `docs/current/achievement-review-tracker.yaml`, and related guidance docs
  - proxy-derived current master-CV representation

Each baseline produced:
- combined fit score
- readiness tier
- score breakdown
- strongest supported signals
- gap analysis
- safe claims / unsafe claims
- representation diagnosis
- curation priorities
- master-CV upgrade actions

## High-Level Findings

### 1. The candidate is competitive across architect and adjacent senior-IC / player-coach categories.

Every completed category landed in `GOOD`.

What is consistently supported:
- enterprise architecture ownership
- modernization plus greenfield platform range
- production AI platform delivery
- retrieval, evaluation, guardrails, structured outputs, and integration depth
- stakeholder translation and player-coach technical leadership
- reliability, observability, CI/CD, and operational discipline

Interpretation:
- there is no evidence that the candidate is a poor fit for the target category set
- the current question is no longer “is there enough evidence?”
- the current question is “how well do generated CVs convert this evidence into job-specific outcomes?”

### 2. The strongest fit is still architect-first and platform-first, but adjacent categories are now equally viable.

The latest results show:
- `ai_architect_*` remains strong and stable
- `head_of_ai_*` remains viable when interpreted as architect-first / player-coach AI leadership
- `staff_ai_engineer_*`, `tech_lead_ai_eea`, and `ai_eng_manager_eea` are now all credible adjacent targets

Interpretation:
- the evidence base supports a broader high-value target set than just architect categories
- but the safe common denominator remains:
  - AI platform architecture
  - production delivery
  - governance / evaluation / reliability
  - technical leadership without executive inflation

### 3. “Head of AI” still does not behave like a literal executive category.

This remains one of the most important findings from the eval work.

Even where `head_of_ai_*` scores are strong, the safe fit is:
- architect-first
- production AI platform leadership
- player-coach or technical AI leader

Not safe to infer:
- VP / C-level framing
- heavy org-building ownership
- hiring-led or budget-led executive identity

Implication:
- use `head_of_ai_*` as adjacency and job-targeting context
- do not let it distort the master CV into executive branding

### 4. The master-CV edits improved adjacent category coverage without materially breaking architect fit.

Latest rerun deltas:
- `ai_architect_global`: `7.90 -> 7.80` (`-0.10`)
- `head_of_ai_global`: `7.70 -> 8.07` (`+0.37`)
- `staff_ai_engineer_eea`: `7.50 -> 7.95` (`+0.45`)

Interpretation:
- the master-CV changes clearly helped staff-level and head-adjacent positioning
- the small architect dip is within a reasonable noise band for model-scored evals and does not change the overall conclusion
- the current edited master CV is acceptable as the Phase 1 baseline

### 5. Unsafe-claim boundaries remain clear and should stay intact.

Across the completed set, the eval consistently penalizes:
- executive AI leadership titles
- org-building-heavy or formal people-management inflation
- budget or P&L claims
- research / publication / RLHF / fine-tuning framing
- exaggerated provider or compliance authority claims

This is still a feature, not a bug.
It keeps the CV optimization path defensible.
