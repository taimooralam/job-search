## Religious & Cultural Fit Assessment

### Swiss Legal Framework

Swiss Federal Constitution protects religious freedom, including the right to pray. However:
- **Prayer breaks are case-by-case.** The employer must protect employee personality (including religious freedom), but the employee's duty of fidelity means religious practice is limited when it affects the employer's overriding business interests.
- **No absolute right** to prayer breaks during work hours — unlike some US/UK frameworks. The employer can refuse if it causes "disruption to the organization or the smooth operation of the business."
- **Fasting (Ramadan):** Employer can react if fasting affects work performance. Otherwise employer may be liable in case of accident.
- **Source:** Swiss Federal Constitution + espacedroit.ch (Swiss employment law reference)

#### Verdict

The **timing advice is mostly sound**: do **not** raise religious-practice logistics in the April 2, 2026 CEO call unless directly asked. The current framing is directionally correct, but it misses a few Swiss-startup-context risks.

2. **The legal framing should be softer.**

5. **Ask for a quiet space, not special treatment.**  
   In a Swiss startup context, the strongest framing is simple and operational.
